## Be Careful: `.bluesky.bot` Usernames Do Not Guarantee Authenticity {{ date: '2025-05-02T00:00Z' }}

On **Bluesky.bot**, we allow users to create personalized subdomains ending with `.bluesky.bot`, like `openai.bluesky.bot`, to make sharing their Bluesky profile easier.

However, it is **very important to understand** that this naming system **does _not_ verify or confirm the authenticity of any account**.

### People using Bluesky.bot may not be the real ones

To be clear:
**Anyone** can register any available name under `.bluesky.bot`. There is **no identity verification**, brand validation, or manual approval process involved.

> A username like `openai.bluesky.bot` does _not_ mean it’s actually OpenAI.

For example:

- `openai.bluesky.bot` could be _anyone_ — not necessarily OpenAI.
- `elonmusk.bluesky.bot` may have _no connection_ to Elon Musk.
- `yourgovernment.bluesky.bot` is _not necessarily_ an official entity.

### Why we allow these names

The `.bluesky.bot` system was designed to offer a simple and flexible way for users to create shareable profile links.
It is **not a verification system**, and should never be interpreted as one.

### How to identify official accounts

To recognize real or verified accounts on Bluesky:

- Look for **custom domain handles** (like `@openai.com`) that are verified within the Bluesky platform.
- Cross-check the handle through **official websites or social media** of the organization or individual.
- **Never rely solely on a `.bluesky.bot` subdomain** as proof of identity.

### In summary

- `.bluesky.bot` usernames are customizable and open to everyone.
- **They do not prove identity or authenticity.**
- Always be cautious when interacting with profiles, and verify through trusted sources.

If you have any questions, please do not hesitate to contact [Bluesky's moderation service](https://bsky.app/profile/moderation.bsky.app) directly on the platform.

2. **Data Transfer:**

3. **Interoperability:**
   The protocol’s design allows for seamless data and identity transfers between different platforms. This interoperability is achieved through standardized data formats and authentication methods.
